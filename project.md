## Principles

- **Very minimalistic.** Lightweight pages, near-zero JS, quiet design, boring tooling. Nothing to babysit between posts.
- **Memoir tone.** Entries are about our life with pizza in it, not consumer-guide reviews.
- **Structured opening.** Every entry starts with the review list below, then the prose.
- **No scores, ever.** "Beauty: normal" is about as damning as it gets.

## The review list

Every entry opens with this list. Fields are fixed; values are free-form prose. A field can be left empty rather than forced. (In the site, these live in each review's front-matter `[review]` table and are rendered by the template — the archetype scaffolds them for you.)

- Style:
- Base:
- Crown:
- Cheese:
- Tomatoes:
- Olive oil:
- Leoparding & char flavour:
- Alveolation:
- Beauty:

Example (a real one):

> - Style: neapolitan
> - Base: overall tasty, dense but not too heavy, very crispy, proper level
> - Crown: appropriate neopolitan
> - Cheese: good, creamy, well-distributed.
> - Tomatoes: very good, pungent, sweet and umami
> - Olive oil: tasted like nothing, both the table olive oil and the one the pizza
> - Leoparding & char flavour
> - Alveolation
> - Beauty: normal

## How it's built

A small **custom Hugo theme** (no third-party theme dependency), deployed to **GitHub Pages** via the official Actions workflow, with a custom domain to come. Photos live in each review's own folder (a Hugo page bundle) and are auto-resized to WebP by an image render hook in `layouts/_default/_markup/`.

We evaluated the `hugo-bearblog` theme (rationale in [research.md](research.md)) but wrote our own tiny theme instead — it's less code than bending bearblog to this masthead + list, and everything lives in `layouts/`.
