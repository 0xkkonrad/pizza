It was a Tuesday, which is the correct day for pizza — nobody chooses a Tuesday for an occasion, so a good one on a Tuesday is a small piece of luck rather than a plan. We walked twenty minutes in the last of the light and got the last table, the wobbly one by the pass.

The crown was the star and knew it — tall, freckled, faintly smoky. The middle couldn't keep up: the base gave way under the cheese and we ate the last third with a fork, which we have decided is not a defeat but a technique. Natalia forgave it everything for the oil, which was green and sharp and clearly chosen by someone who tastes their own food.

We didn't rank it against Naples, and that felt like the point. Different day, different weather, different marriage-mood. We split the bill, walked home the long way, and agreed only that we'd come back — probably on a Tuesday.
