Naples, Italy

Da Michele

by Konrad & Natalia · March 2026

We queued for fifty minutes in a rain that never quite decided to fall. Natalia had formed opinions about the queue long before we reached the door — about the couple ahead of us splitting one margherita "to stay light," about the man photographing his pizza from a chair he'd dragged into the street. By the time we sat down at a shared marble table we were starving, and starving is the best sauce, which rather complicates the reviewing.

The Particulars

Style
Neapolitan.
Base
Overall tasty, dense but not too heavy, very crispy, proper level.
Crown
Appropriate Neapolitan.
Cheese
Good, creamy, well-distributed.
Tomatoes
Very good, pungent, sweet and umami.
Olive oil
Tasted like nothing — both the table oil and the one on the pizza.
Leoparding & char
There, but shy; more spot than smoke.
Alveolation
Open and uneven, in the good way.
Beauty
Normal.

It came fast, folded, on a paper plate, and it was very good in the plain, unbothered way the great ones are. The base did all the work. The oil did nothing at all — we kept adding it, hoping, the way you do. We ate quickly, said little, and walked out into the same undecided rain, already arguing gently about where it ranked.
