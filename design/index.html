Where this leaves us

The Broadsheet is the direction. It keeps everything the research pointed to — memoir voice, the nine-field list as a proper factbox, feather-light and quiet — and now genuinely reads as a newspaper, on warm newsprint that never goes dark.

Two small calls I made that are easy to change — tell me your preference:

  1. Headline style. Right now the place name is the headline ("Da Michele") with a descriptive deck under it. The alternative is a real newspaper headline ("A Margherita That Lets the Base Do the Talking") with the place in the kicker. I went with place-as-headline so the archive stays scannable.
  2. The numbering & furniture. I leaned into the bit — "Vol. I / No. 1," "Price · One Slice," "Continued on P. 2," the "▪" end mark. Dial it up (more front-page cosplay) or down (cleaner, just masthead + columns) to taste.
