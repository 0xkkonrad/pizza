We queued for fifty minutes in a rain that never quite decided to fall. Natalia had formed opinions about the queue long before we reached the door — about the couple ahead of us splitting one margherita "to stay light," about the man photographing his pizza from a chair he'd dragged into the street.

By the time we sat down at a shared marble table we were starving, and starving is the best sauce, which rather complicates the reviewing.

It came fast, folded, on a paper plate, and it was very good in the plain, unbothered way the great ones are. The base did all the work. The oil did nothing at all — we kept adding it, hoping, the way you do. We ate quickly, said little, and walked out into the same undecided rain, already arguing gently about where it ranked.
