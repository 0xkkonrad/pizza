# Research

Two parts: the landscape of existing pizza-review sites (informs positioning + name), and the stack/theme research (informs the build). Compiled 2026-07-05.

## Part 1 — The pizza-review landscape

*Live-verified facts are stated plainly; anything not confirmed against a live page during this research is marked **(unverified)** and worth a quick re-check before you rely on it.*

### 2. Small personal / memoir-style blogs — and what makes them charming

The memoir lane is thinly populated; its exemplars are either archives or low-cadence Blogspot lifers — and **no sustained static-site (Hugo/Jekyll/Bear) pizza log was found at all** (Bear Blog discover and Hacker News searches came up empty). That corner of the niche is genuinely open.

- **Slice Harvester** (above) remains the archetype: one fixed ritual (plain slice, every pizzeria, one borough), life bleeding into the reviews, a real narrative arc with an ending. Its afterlife as a Simon & Schuster memoir is the genre's proof of worth.
- **Chicago Pizza Club** — http://chicagopizzaclub.blogspot.com/ — a friend group's "blog and public record": sequentially numbered meetings since ~2004 (Meeting #142, Jan 2026). That's ~7 posts a year sustained for two decades — proof that **numbering entries turns a sub-monthly cadence into an institution**. **Active.**
- **Pizza Quixote** — https://mainlinepizzaquest.blogspot.com/ — solo quest blog since 2011; no scores, the verdict is whether it's "destination pizza worth the trip"; frozen supermarket pies get the same gravity as legendary pizzerias. **Active** (May 2026 post), 15 years on free Blogspot.
- **New York Pizza Project** — https://nypizzaproject-blog.tumblr.com/ — five friends documenting classic slice-joint *people* (photos + oral history, no ratings); charming precisely because it documents rather than ranks. Dormant.
- **Worst Pizza** — https://www.worstpizza.com/ — great inverted premise ("find the best by sorting through the worst," 0–8 slices) hollowed out by PR-ish posts and an "Advertise" link; dormant since Jan 2024. The cautionary tale.
- **Cheese and Biscuits** — http://cheesenbiscuits.blogspot.com/ — Chris Pople's London restaurant diary since 2007, still active (Jun 2026): witty, /10 scores, frank about bad meals, discloses comps, no ads, default Blogspot. 19 years of one person logging dinners.
- Couple-run food blogs (the closest structural kin to a two-author blog): **A Couple Cooks** — https://www.acouplecooks.com — genuinely two-voiced in origin, now a large SEO recipe business; **Pinch of Yum** — https://pinchofyum.com — actually single-voiced (Lindsay writes; husband Bjork runs the business and appears as a recurring character); **White on Rice Couple** — https://whiteonricecouple.com — the most genuinely two-person of the canon. Every canonical couple food blog became a business, and none does *reviews* — a tiny couple-voiced review blog has no incumbent.
- Voice-first memoir food blogs (the tone benchmark): **Orangette** (Molly Wizenberg) — https://orangette.net — farewell post Jan 2022, the gold standard of diary-as-food-writing; her memoir ***Delancey* is literally about a couple opening a pizza restaurant** — required reading for this project. **The Wednesday Chef** (Luisa Weiss) — https://www.thewednesdaychef.com — dormant ("RIP!" per her own bio), continued on Substack. **Smitten Kitchen** (Deb Perelman) — https://smittenkitchen.com — still very active and still essentially one person: domestic anecdote first, then dinner. **101 Cookbooks** (Heidi Swanson) — https://www.101cookbooks.com — the founding constraint is the name; minimalist aesthetic discipline.
- Project-constraint logs (nearest spiritual kin to a fixed attribute list): **Sandwich Tribunal** — https://www.sandwichtribunal.com — eating through Wikipedia's entire List of Sandwiches since 2014; finished the alphabet in 2020 ("The End"), then simply started over — now on pass three. Very active, no ads. **Snack Stack** — https://snackstack.net — essayistic snack history. A tight, slightly absurd constraint sustains a tiny audience indefinitely.

What the charming ones share, concretely: (1) a **fixed ritual or constraint** stated up front that generates every post — the constraint *is* the brand, and no post ever needs a justification; (2) a **running count or numbered ritual** (Meeting #142; 435 slices; "The 5 Best Pizzas I Ate in 2025") — numbering makes a slow cadence read as devotion, not neglect; (3) **life context in the reviews** — the pizza is the occasion, the diary is the content; (4) **no commercial shape** — every monetization step observed in the wild (ads links, SEO reshaping, PR posts) drained the charm; the survivors post only when they feel like it; (5) **cheap, unfashionable, durable platforms** — the beloved ones have run default Blogspot for 15–20 years; (6) **a small recurring cast** — readers follow people, not pizzas.

### 3. Rating formats across the space

- **Single overall score**: One Bite's 0–10.x (https://onebite.app); Slice Harvester's 0–8 slices. Dominant in populist reviewing.
- **Crowd stars**: Yelp https://www.yelp.com, Google Maps https://www.google.com/maps, TripAdvisor https://www.tripadvisor.com — 5-star averages, no structure.
- **Ranked jury lists, no scores**: 50 Top Pizza (https://www.50toppizza.it).
- **Symbol tiers on structured guide entries**: Gambero Rosso's spicchi/rotelle (https://www.gamberorosso.it) — the closest thing to institutional attribute-thinking, but published as a tier + blurb, not an attribute list.
- **Certification rubric (spec, not review)**: AVPN's Neapolitan disciplinare — https://www.pizzanapoletana.org — literally specifies cornicione height (1–2 cm), oven temp (~430–480°C) and 60–90 s bake. A spec sheet, but for certifying makers, not reviewing pies.
- **Per-component prose critique**: pizzamaking.com forum threads and r/pizza (https://www.reddit.com/r/pizza/) routinely dissect crumb, bake and char of home pies; Italian pizza-nerd culture has the exact vocabulary we use — *cornicione, alveolatura, leopardatura* — in forums like La Confraternita della Pizza (https://www.laconfraternitadellapizza.net, unverified) and Italian review blogs (unverified specifics). But this lives in comment threads, not as a review format.
- **Component-scored reviews (sub-scores per attribute)**: a real but minority pattern, concentrated in **dead hobby blogs** — Pizza Grotto (https://pizzagrotto.blogspot.com/, dead 2009), Syracuse Pizza Geek (https://pizzageek.wordpress.com/, dormant 2011), The Frozen Pizza Standard (https://frozenpizzastandard.wordpress.com/, dead 2015), Seattle Pizza Coalition (https://seattlepizzacoalition.com/about/, dead 2017) — plus competition jury sheets (Campionato della Pizza scheda: https://www.scattidigusto.it/campionato-della-pizza-2026-le-regole). Tellingly, where these blocks exist they sit at the **end** of reviews, and the durable independents moved the other way: NEPA Pizza Review abandoned sub-scores for a single 1–10, and The Pizza Snob (https://thepizzasnob.net/rating-system/) rejects component grading on principle.
- **Attribute-list-first reviews**: **zero pizza examples found, US or Italian** — nobody opens the review with a structured sensory block before the prose. The convention we're importing is standard *elsewhere*: specialty-coffee cupping forms (SCA — https://sca.coffee), wine's WSET Systematic Approach to Tasting (https://www.wsetglobal.com), fragrance note pyramids (https://www.fragrantica.com) — and, within pizza media, only *equipment* reviews open with spec tables. The Style/Base/Crown/Cheese/Tomatoes/Olive oil/Leoparding/Alveolation/Beauty header would be immediately recognizable to the Italian-forum subculture yet first-of-kind among pizza review blogs. (Caveat: proving a negative is impossible — gated Facebook scorecard groups and non-indexed blogs can't be ruled out.)

### 5. Positioning takeaways

1. **The memoir lane is open.** Populist scoring (One Bite) and institutional lists (50 Top Pizza, Gambero Rosso) are saturated, and social video owns the mass audience; but the personal-diary lane has been essentially vacant since Slice Harvester ended (2011) — today's survivors (I Dream of Pizza, Pizza Quixote, Chicago Pizza Club) are earnest prose diaries on 2000s Blogspot, none structured, none static-site. A tiny, non-commercial, slow site isn't a handicap in this genre — every charming exemplar was exactly that, and the ones that died, died of turning into content businesses.
2. **The attribute block is the signature — freeze it.** Nobody in pizza leads with a fixed spec sheet; the nearest kin are certification rubrics and coffee/wine tasting forms. Its power comes from repetition: same nine fields, same order, every post, forever (like Hagendorf's plain slice or Sandwich Tribunal's list). Resist adding fields; the constraint is the brand.
3. **Spec sheet + diary, and consider no overall score.** The pizza writing people still cite years later (Slice Harvester, Scott's Pizza Journal) wins on voice and life-context, not numbers. Let the attribute list carry rigor and comparability, let the prose carry the marriage, the city, the evening — and letting "Beauty" be the only holistic axis differentiates from every 0–10 and 5-star system in the space. The graveyard evidence supports this: every component-*scored* pizza blog found is dead (2009–2017), while the survivors use one number or none — descriptive free-form fields with no score sidestep the pattern entirely. Bonus move to steal from Chicago Pizza Club: number the entries (No. 1, No. 2, …) so a slow cadence reads as an institution.
4. **On names: hard-avoid Pizza Diary/Diaries; skip Pizza Journal, Cornicione and Undercarriage; prefer ownable over vocabulary.** The Pizza Diaries is an active exact-name blog; Pizza Journal is Wiener's turf; Cornicione is a pizzeria name worldwide; Undercarriage reads as Portnoy (and its .pizza is squatted). The craft terms signal exactly the right subculture but are generic vocabulary — unsearchable and unownable — with Alveolation the exception (verified clean everywhere). The verified-clean distinctive phrases (**Crown & Char, Alveolation, Slice Notes, We Ate Pizza**) are the strongest candidates; "We Ate Pizza" additionally says *couple* and *diary* in three words. Whichever gets picked, re-check the Instagram handle right before buying.

## Part 2 — Stack & theme

Scope: memoir-style two-person pizza blog, <1 post/month, GitHub Pages, public repo. Requirements: very light, ~zero JS, quiet design, good prose typography, a few photos per review, each post opens with a fixed markdown list (Style / Base / Crown / Cheese / Tomatoes / Olive oil / Leoparding & char flavour / Alveolation / Beauty). All maintenance data checked against the repos on 2026-07-05; page weights measured on the live demos (HTML + CSS + JS, uncompressed, images excluded).

### Theme shortlist

#### Hugo Bear Blog — `janraasch/hugo-bearblog`
[Repo](https://github.com/janraasch/hugo-bearblog) · [Demo](https://janraasch.github.io/hugo-bearblog/) · last push **2026-06-30**, 1.5k★, active
Weight: **~6 KB/page, zero JS** (verified: 0 `<script>` tags), ~1.7 KB CSS inlined in `<head>`, system fonts, automatic dark mode via `prefers-color-scheme`.
**Pros:** the proven "quiet memoir blog" look (Bear Blog aesthetic); the most actively maintained of the truly tiny themes; whole design lives in one `style.html` partial, trivial to tweak; markdown images render clean and full-width.
**Cons:** no built-in image resizing (true of all six — see Photos below); post list is plain titles+dates, no thumbnails; very few knobs by design.

#### Etch — `LukasJoswiak/etch`
[Repo](https://github.com/LukasJoswiak/etch) · [Demo](https://lukasjoswiak.github.io/etch/) · last push **2026-05-08**, 302★, active
Weight: **~8 KB/page, zero JS** (1.8 KB HTML + 6.3 KB CSS), no webfonts, auto dark mode.
**Pros:** explicitly built "with a focus on writing" — the best prose typography of the shortlist out of the box; i18n-ready; zero external dependencies.
**Cons:** small community; docs live in a GitHub wiki; slightly plainer chrome than bearblog (no footer/nav niceties until you add them).

#### Bear Cub — `clente/hugo-bearcub`
[Repo](https://github.com/clente/hugo-bearcub) · [Demo](https://clente.github.io/hugo-bearcub/) · last push **2025-08-22**, 308★, slow-but-alive (it's "finished" software)
Weight: **~8 KB/page, zero JS** (5.6 KB HTML + 2.1 KB CSS), ships a strict `default-src 'none'` CSP.
**Pros:** bearblog fork with better SEO/OpenGraph, auto-generated social-share cards, multilingual support — nice if reviews get shared in chats.
**Cons:** ~11 months since last commit and a fraction of upstream's community; tracks upstream bearblog loosely.

#### Archie — `athul/archie`
[Repo](https://github.com/athul/archie) · [Demo](https://athul.github.io/archie) · last push **2026-03-30**, 1.4k★, active-ish (41 open issues/PRs)
Weight: ~30 KB code + ~2 KB theme-toggle JS + **three Google Fonts families loaded from the CDN** (≈70–100 KB extra, third-party requests).
**Pros:** pretty out of the box, dark-mode toggle, tidy list pages.
**Cons:** webfont CDN calls (weight + privacy; self-hosting them is extra work); needs JS for the toggle; monospace-accented "developer blog" look fits a memoir less well.

#### Paper — `nanxiaobei/hugo-paper`
[Repo](https://github.com/nanxiaobei/hugo-paper) · [Demo](https://hugo-paper.vercel.app) · last push **2026-03-09**, 2.4k★, active
Weight: ~64 KB (7 KB HTML + **55.5 KB Tailwind CSS** + ~1 KB inline JS), plus a Gravatar fetch by default.
**Pros:** polished and genuinely minimal-looking; dark mode; biggest community of the six.
**Cons:** heaviest here; templates are dense Tailwind utility soup, so adjusting typography means fighting Tailwind; aesthetic is "modern app landing", not memoir.

#### XMin — `yihui/hugo-xmin`
[Repo](https://github.com/yihui/hugo-xmin) · [Demo](https://xmin.yihui.org) · last push **2026-06-08**, 966★, maintained for 8+ years
Weight: theme core is **~1.2 KB CSS, zero JS** — the lightest possible. (The demo homepage measures ~300 KB only because its sample pages load KaTeX for math; the theme itself adds none of that.)
**Pros:** ~150 lines total, a blank canvas you fully own and can never be broken by upstream.
**Cons:** it's a starting point, not a product — you write your own figure styling, nav, 404, etc. Only right if tinkering is part of the fun.

**Checked and cut:** `colorchestra/smol` (last push 2024-08-11 — dormant); riggraz's *no-style-please* is **Jekyll-only** — the Hugo port `hanwenguo/hugo-theme-nostyleplease` (last push 2025-01-20) is semi-dormant and deliberately unstyled, which treats photos poorly; `hugo-sid/hugo-blog-awesome` is very active (pushed 2026-07-02) but ~50 KB + toggle JS and more chrome than this blog needs.

### Recommendation

**Winner: Hugo Bear Blog** (`janraasch/hugo-bearblog`). It hits every requirement with no compromise: ~6 KB pages, literally zero JavaScript, system fonts, auto dark mode, and the calm Bear aesthetic that already defines this genre of small personal blog. It is also the most actively maintained tiny theme (commits within the last week), and its single-partial CSS makes the inevitable "slightly bigger photos, slightly wider measure" tweaks a 10-minute job.

**Runner-up: Etch.** Marginally nicer prose typography out of the box and equally dependency-free, so it's the pick if the reading experience matters more than the Bear look. The only real risks are its small community and wiki-hosted docs; the theme surface is small enough that abandonment would barely matter. (If share-cards in group chats are wanted, Bear Cub is the bearblog variant that adds them.)

The fixed rating list needs no theme support at all: put it in `archetypes/reviews.md` (front matter + the nine-line `- **Style:** …` list), and `hugo new reviews/2026-07-da-michele/index.md` pre-fills every new review.

### Deploy (GitHub Pages, current practice)

Use the **official GitHub Actions flow**, not a `gh-pages` branch: Settings → Pages → Source: **GitHub Actions**, then commit the workflow from [Hugo's official guide](https://gohugo.io/host-and-deploy/host-on-github-pages/) — `actions/checkout` → `actions/configure-pages` → build with a pinned `HUGO_VERSION` → `actions/upload-pages-artifact` → `actions/deploy-pages`. Never commit `public/`. The older `peaceiris/actions-hugo` + `actions-gh-pages` pair still works but is the legacy branch-push method; the artifact flow is what both Hugo and GitHub document today and needs no deploy branch or token juggling.

**Custom domain:** enter it under Settings → Pages → Custom domain — with Actions-based publishing a `CNAME` file is ignored and not needed ([GitHub docs](https://docs.github.com/en/pages/configuring-a-custom-domain-for-your-github-pages-site/managing-a-custom-domain-for-your-github-pages-site)). DNS: apex → A records `185.199.108.153 / 185.199.109.153 / 185.199.110.153 / 185.199.111.153` (optionally AAAA `2606:50c0:8000::153`–`8003::153`, or ALIAS/ANAME to `<user>.github.io`); `www` → CNAME `<user>.github.io`. Verify the domain in account settings (prevents takeover), tick **Enforce HTTPS**, and set the same URL as `baseURL` in `hugo.toml`.

### Photos

Keep each review as a **page bundle** — `content/reviews/2026-07-da-michele/index.md` with its photos in the same folder — and add one small [image render hook](https://gohugo.io/render-hooks/images/) (`layouts/_default/_markup/render-image.html`) that grabs the page resource and emits a resized derivative: `{{ $img := .Page.Resources.Get .Destination }}{{ $small := $img.Resize "1400x webp q75" }}` plus explicit `width`/`height` attributes and `loading="lazy" decoding="async"`. That way posts keep plain `![Crust](crust.jpg)` markdown, while multi-MB phone JPEGs become ~100–200 KB WebP at build time with no CLS and no JS ([Hugo image processing](https://gohugo.io/content-management/image-processing/) resizes and converts natively — WebP is the sweet spot; AVIF is also supported now but slower to encode for negligible gain at these sizes). Export originals at ≤2000 px long edge before committing so the public repo stays small, and don't bother caching `resources/_gen` in CI — at a few photos a month, rebuild time is irrelevant.
