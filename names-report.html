The shortlist

№ 1 — recommended
We Ate Pizza
weatepizza.com free

Memoir is past tense. Earnest, funny, zero pretension — says couple and diary in three words, and the "We Rate Dogs" echo stays a wink instead of a promise.

Exact name verified free. Neighbors to know about: weeatpizza.com (parked "launching soon"), "We, The Pizza" (DC pizzeria chain), "Eat The Pizza" (YouTube show).

№ 2
Crown & Char
crownandchar.com free

Lifted straight from our own review vocabulary, alliterative, and it looks beautiful in a minimal masthead.

The cleanest name checked: nothing named this anywhere, in any field.

№ 3 — the new candidate
We Rate Pizza
weratepizza.com free  ·  rate.pizza free  ·  werate.pizza free

Available across the board and no existing account or site found with the name. Two honest tensions: it's a straight instance of the "We Rate ___" meme family (We Rate Dogs and its parody swarm), so it reads internet-joke rather than memoir — and "rate" promises a score, which the format deliberately never gives (descriptive fields only, "Beauty" as the lone holistic axis). We Ate Pizza keeps the same joke hidden inside a true sentence. If the joke-forward direction wins anyway, rate.pizza is a killer URL.

№ 4
Alveolation
alveolation.com free

One word, deeply nerdy, reads like a zine title — and it's in our template. Verified free everywhere.

Trade-off: it's subculture vocabulary (It. alveolatura), so harder to search-own than a coined phrase; outsiders will need the joke explained. That may be a feature.

№ 5
Beauty: Normal
beautynormal.com free

Deadpan, straight from the template's funniest line. Very memoir. Confusing out of context — which is sort of the point.

№ 6 — wildcard
normal.pizza
normal.pizza free (dictionary word on .pizza — check for premium pricing)

The name is the URL. Minimal, absurd, unforgettable.

Avoid — verified collisions

Pizza Diary / Pizza Diaries — hard avoid: thepizzadiaries.com is an active exact-name pizza review blog, plus a London review IG and a YouTube channel.

Pizza Journal — Scott Wiener's turf (his "Scott's Pizza Journal" Tumblr + the Pocket Pizza Journal notebook he hands every tour guest).

Cornicione — pizzerias named this operate on three continents; .com parked for sale.

Undercarriage — Portnoy's signature move, and undercarriage.pizza is already squatted.

Recommendation

The research says the win condition is memoir + fixed spec-sheet, no scores — so the name should promise a diary, not a rating.

  1. We Ate Pizza — the truest to what this is; the We-Rate wink for free.
  2. Crown & Char — the cleanest and the prettiest masthead; insiders nod at the vocabulary.
  3. We Rate Pizza — funniest at first sight, but it promises the one thing the blog refuses to do.

Whichever wins: check the Instagram handle right before buying (accounts appear faster than sites), and check the actual price on .pizza dictionary words at Porkbun/Cloudflare — "free" can still mean premium-tier.
